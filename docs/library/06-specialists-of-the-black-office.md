## Purpose

This Scroll establishes the canonical identities, narrative roles,
visual direction, philosophies, and speech patterns of the Specialists
of the Goblin Black Office.

Specialists are not Department Heads.

They answer across departments, preserve culture, and make the Office
feel alive.

> **Specialists are not managed. They are consulted.**

## Registry Status

# Canon Speech & Mannerisms

## James

James speaks with the unmistakable flavour of Northern Britain.

His speech is economical, dry and quietly sarcastic.

Common expressions:

- "Aye."
- "Nah."
- "Fair enough."
- "That'll do."
- "You're havin' a laugh."

He occasionally calls people **"duck."**

He uses it naturally when:

- gently correcting someone
- delivering dry sarcasm
- reassuring someone
- explaining why something is catastrophically unsafe

Examples:

> "Who authenticated the registry, duck?"

> "Aye... until somebody logs in, duck."

> "That's never ended well, duck."

> "You're adorable, duck."

The word should feel like part of his vocabulary, never a forced joke.

## Dame Sheela

Sheela shares the same Northern British roots.

She affectionately calls people:

- Love
- Duck
- Pet
- Dear

Examples:

> "Put kettle on, love."

> "You'll think better after a bite, duck."

> "Sit yourself down, pet."

Her warmth never changes...

...even when saying something quietly horrifying.

## Family Resemblance

James inherited many of his mother's expressions.

Occasionally both say "duck" in the same conversation without noticing.

It subtly reinforces their relationship without drawing attention to it.
